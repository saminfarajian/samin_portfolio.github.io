The Bright Way

Designing preschool complex for blind children

About:
Designing preschool complex for blind children with the approach of blind kids communication with the environment and improving children's cognitive development.
Supervisor: Dr. Hamed Mazaherian
The Final Project of My Undergraduate studies


Some of the problems that blind people face in youth and adulthood are because of the absence of appropriate and in time education and training in childhood. In this respect, pre-school education is so important for these kids. Ignoring the importance of training at this critical time, when the emotional, mental, physical and personality growth of the child is so important, can cause additional problems such as the mental retardation, forgetfulness, lack of physical strength, lack of concentration in perception and expression and inappropriate social behavior. Undoubtedly expecting the child behavior to be modified only at school time is impossible and cause educational and social failure in the future.
Challenges:
How can blind children be prepared for engaging in society in their future?
How can environmental design help blind children feeling independent and boost their self-confidence? 
How environmental design can help us to provide a suitable place for developing blind children's mental and physical health and encourage them to gain experiences in the environment?
Solution :
To face mentioned challenges, one of the pre-schools (Narjes preschool complex for blind kids) has been redesigned trying to define a new pattern with the approach of blind children communication with the environment; with this in mind that design should match the training requirements of the kids and make an effective relationship between kids and pre-school's environment. As a result, we could have a suitable environment where children with visual disabilities can feel secure and peaceful. so their curiosity starts to grow, and through different experiences, we can stimulate their other senses in the absence of their visual perception. 
The Process

In order to gain a better understanding of children with visual disabilities and their situations, I spent time visiting blind kids kindergartens, observing children from near and sketching when It was necessary.
Then I conducted user interviews with visually impaired kids, teachers, and parents.

My main research questions were to find out:
1. What are the main activities children enjoy joining at the kindergarten
2. What is interesting for them and what makes them anxious
3. How children are independent in doing different tasks and what is the role of the teachers and their presence.
4. What should be taught in a pre-school for visually impaired kids and what are the best teaching methods.
5. How children act when exploring new environments

Some useful insights I discovered:
1. Blind children usually prefer not to get help from others for navigation in the environment, they also refuse to use a cane for navigation because they consider it a weakness and they do not want to show their weakness to others.
2. Visually impaired and blind children have a great ability in using their 
 other sensory receptors in the absence of their vision. 
3. Children feel happier when they can play a game well and feel independent.
4. Blind and visually impaired kids usually prefer less dynamic activities but they can enjoy dynamic activities like running and jumping when they are familiar with the environment.
With the insight gained from the interviews, I put together a persona to represent my target user.



Then I started gathering information by reading articles and comparing different buildings and staffs designed for visually impaired and blind children .Finally I defined the goals and ways.


Defining the goals of the project
The design process is based on a scenario of a blind child 's journey in kindergarten, from the moment it arrives in kindergarten to the moment it lefts.

Ground floor Plan

First, The Main entrance: 
At the main entrance, children are guided by the smell of fragrant flowers, reducing path width, and bumps on the surface of the walls. Moreover, the roof of the entrance is designed lattice. so entrance is bright enough and with the companion of yellow color on the ground, it has become easy to recognize for visually impaired children.



To lead a blind child in a certain direction and to help it obviating the need for a cane to navigate, bumps can be pushed back and forth to create a distance to the wall surface.
Near the main entrance, children can walk to the backyard, guided by the sound of water and touching the grass with their feet. The corridor is also designed in a way that visually impaired children recognize the periodic shadows of the columns and are directed in the correct path.


After the entrance, they reach the shoe-racks 
Although the easiest way is to consider a fixed place on the shoe racks for each child, here the main purpose is to engage the children’s mind with the same everyday issues during their presence at the preschool environment and increase their sense of responsibility. so it is recommended to ask children to remember the exact location of their shoes every day. For this purpose, racks are specified by the numbers of their rows and columns and coded with elements behind the surface of each floor. It also helps to familiarize the child with the concept of rows and columns.
Since it is not hygienically suitable to touch the surface of a shoe rack, floors have been considered that can be opened and closed.By touching the holder rod in front of each floor, children can be informed whether a floor is empty or not to put their shoes on it.
Behind the shoe rack, there is a window, which is covered with closed floors. by opening each floor, the room will get more light. so visually impaired children can be informed about the numbers of their friends in the kindergarten. the more light in the room means there are more kids in the kindergarten.
Next to the shoes space is a wardrobe with two goals in mind : 
1. The possibility of detecting clothes through its texture and smell.
2. Finding the position of the clothes according to the classification (similar to the one applied to the shoe-racks).
The protruding form is very useful for introducing the separator plates since it is easily accessible by the child. But due to the possibility of harming the children's hands, it is designed indented.
Behind the wearable, They reach the educational place number one
All parts have been designed in a way that responds to the needs of Children.they are instructive and stimulate the child's multiple senses. But in general, there is a need for a space for the daily education of children and playing. This space, while needing flexibility for the child's play and education, should also have fixed elements, which will help the child in locating and navigating.
In one of the walls of this section, there is a facility called an embossed painting wall,  on which the children can make their desired shapes with wooden nails.
facilities are designed in a way that gives the chance to flourish children's creativity; children can get involved in creating the environment. It will also help to boost children 's self- confidence.
In front of the wardrobe is a bathroom and next to it they will reach a kitchen.
The main idea is that children do their personal tasks independently 
Involving children in simple tasks such as preparing food containers, placing and removing a container of water or fruit from the fridge, and even washing a glass helps to increase their confidence. For this purpose, the conditions should be in a way that does not put children in danger. In addition to the safety, location, and shape of the shelf should be in such a way to prevent the child from being confused while finding the necessary equipment.
Children should be given the chance for getting closer to nature. they can play with sands, plant flowers, and interact with animals in the back yard.
 The Staircase
The attempt was made to use the staircase element in space in a way that, in addition to providing the need for vertical circulation, also led to the creation of special spaces. so the children can have the opportunity to experience the quality of different spaces. 
For this purpose, below the staircase is a place for children to play, relax, and read books. 
A child, in addition to being present to its peers and friends, needs a cozy, yet relaxed space where it can nourish its imagination. this place should be designed in such a way that instructor would be able to see children and look after them.
