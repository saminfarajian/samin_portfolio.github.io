Designing Tourism Complex

Concertvatiating on people's engagement with building it is perceived that every building has nugatory spaces fully designed but not used. what is the reason?  Getting familiar with people and their basic physical and mental needs, architectures are more able to design buildings that are more suitable for people, regarding their functions.
Challenge 
How can we design buildings that are due to people's behavior in spaces? how can we provide spaces for people to increase their social interactions? how can people engage in the process of the design as not just consumers but also designers of the space?
About
MeloMa Tourism complex is a highway rest place located North of Qom on the Qom-Tehran Highway near the tourist cities: Isfahan, Tehran, Qom, and Kashan. consisting of Coffe shops, traditional restaurants, and shops, MeloMa aims to provide spaces for tourisms and people to interact, rest and have fun during their trips.
By studying the existing buildings we can understand the people's behavior in spaces and by explicating the data we are able to design buildings more efficiently due to people's need which leads to a reduction of unused and nugatory spaces and therefore saving money, time and energy.

"Milad Noor" is one of the oldest shopping malls in Tehran. It had a revolutionary design in its time and it has been facing the highest average of time spent in by people in comparison to other malls. we can extract the design factors that make "Milad Noor" favorable for people to spend time and interact with each other to utilize in our design.

"Miad Nur" Mall. Tehran, Iran

Reactions of people to buildings are not some random movements but they give us some useful hints of where people want to gather, pass, or wait. therefor by knowing the locations of people and paths they have taken in a building we can behold how people want to be in a space, as a result, by this data, we can design public spaces more efficiently.
Each person has a virtual space surrounding himself as a psychological territory that defines a secured and safe space. this space introduces the minimum volume that a human being can live in. I call it a “cloud”. whenever anyone gets closer than this boundary, depending on his intimacy, the person feels threatened.
people passing by usually try not to get so close to their people's cloud. That is why we do not feel comfortable enough in crowded places.
The moment two people start to communicate, they define a new territory each of them has their own psychological boundary but the union of those two virtual spaces defines the third territory for their group communication By detecting the fusion of clouds passing by communications, we can find out the spaces that more communications have been created. 
the result of tracking people portrays how space should have been connected and designed
By tracking 20 people per day for an hour (locating them every 10 minutes ) in 10 days we will have at least 1200 points in the mall. by analyzing the density of these points in the building, we can find the more important spots and the design factors that have made the Milad Noor desirable for people.
Analyzing data we find that there are more interactions created among people in the voids. in the other words voids and the way they are related to each other, are the key factors in the successful  design of "Milad Nur" mall.
Voids are designed and placed due to the analysis of data providing places for people's communication.
Also voids let in the natural light for the project.

Solar Panels
The project site benefits from a hot desert sun that can be used to generate electricity for various uses in the complex. For this purpose, solar panels were placed on the sloping roof which slope and angle is optimized for  maximizing the sunlight.
